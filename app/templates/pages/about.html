About

The Wait Wait Don't Tell Me! Playing Cards Gallery puts on display a number of Wait Wait... Don't Tell Me! playing cards that had been distributed either as a gift during public radio station donation drives or as prizes for listener contestants very early on in the show's run.

The gallery is part of the larger Wait Wait Stats Project that includes the Wait Wait Stats Page , Wait Wait Stats API , and other projects.

Scanning Process

All of the playing cards were batch scanned using an HP Color LaserJet MFP M277 multi-function device at 1200 DPI (dots per inch) as PNG files with the RGB color profile. Some of the first batches of scans were on using the GNOME Document Scanner program on a laptop running Fedora Workstation 44 while the rest were scanned using Adobe Photoshop running on an Apple M1 Max MacBook Pro.

All of the scanned images were converted to the sRGB color profile in Adobe Photoshop and individual cards cropped and, if necessary, rotated before going through a dust removal process.

The dust removal process only involved removing dust, lint or other detritus that was on the scanning bed and did not remove any blemishes, aging, or other imperfections on the cards as I received them. I used the Spot Healing Brush in Content-Aware mode with the smallest brush size needed in order to minimize the impact of the dust removal.

A border is added to pad out the original scanned image to make the resulting image size consistent while not altering the size and scale of the card itself. This was also done to create a uniform scaled size for all cards for use in this gallery.
